A Visual Storm

Documentary on avant-garde artist Aurelio Caminati
& Wild Atlantic inspiration

About the Film

A documentary portrait of avant-garde Italian master artist Aurelio Caminati (1922–2008), whose prophetic vision & storm force nature spanned six decades through a turbulent post war period.

A Visual Storm features Aurelio Caminati’s postwar journey to Ireland, exploring his provocative interpretation of the Irish landscape, as well as his deep connection to the wild Atlantic coastline.

Set against the dramatic backdrop of baroque intensity of Ireland’s wild Atlantic coastline and rich cultural landscape, the film weaves together the raw, untamed power of nature with this resistance master’s drive to create in a celebration of nature as the ultimate power source.

The film interweaves ocean imagery with the artist’s vision, capturing storms, solitude and creative spark that emerges from nature’s fury.

Featuring Artwork

A curated selection of Caminati’s provocative art work and performances, each piece immersed with human emotion, storms, clouds, wind & water.

Artist In Action

Director Bio

Sinéad Walsh is a filmmaker committed to telling bold, visually rich stories. The documentary A Visual Storm emerged from a need to bring the work of avant garde artist Aurelio Caminati into the mainstream.

A graduate of Trinity College Dublin, Art History & English with Film, Sinéad first made waves with her award-winning visual poem dramatization, The Pot Plant. She later earned an MA in Digital Media Management from INA, Paris (2002). Most recently Sinéad’s work has included community films of children and persons with disabilities.

Special thanks to Caminati Estate and the University of Genoa for granting permission to use original photographed artwork by artist Aurelio Caminati from their archives for the creation and promotion of the film.
